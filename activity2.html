History


Dr. The concept of Vidyarthi Sahaya was planted in 1955 from the concept of Achyut Shankar Apte, a great social worker. While studying in France, he experienced that the citizens and the government help the needy students for their education. This brought to his attention the country's approach to education and reflected on the state of education in India at that time. After returning to India, talking to like-minded people, five rural students were initially provided with food in Pune. The construction of hostels with accommodation and food arrangements for the needy in the rural areas and for the aspiring students who want to prove themselves by coming to Pune for higher education was realized from this concept. The name of this concept is 'Vidyarthi Sahayyak Samiti'.
